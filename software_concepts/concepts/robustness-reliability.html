Robustness vs Reliability — A4 Handout

Definitions

Robustness — ability to tolerate unexpected inputs, environmental variation, or partial failures without catastrophic behavior.

Reliability — probability the system performs its required function without failure for a specified period under stated conditions (e.g., uptime, MTBF).

Side by Side Comparison

Practical Interaction

  • Robustness reduces unexpected failures, which improves observed reliability.
  • Reliability engineering ensures predictable availability through redundancy and monitoring.
  • Both are complementary: robust components make reliability measures easier to achieve and maintain.

When to Prioritize

  • Robustness — prioritize when inputs are untrusted, environments vary, or safety is critical.
  • Reliability — prioritize when continuous availability and SLAs matter.

Testing Checklist

  1. Run fuzzing and fault injection to validate robustness.
  2. Execute long‑duration load and availability tests for reliability metrics.
  3. Monitor production for error rates, MTBF, and uptime; iterate on both design and operations.
